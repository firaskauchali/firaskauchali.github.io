Extreme
Weather

The brief for this project is to develop a strategy for a specific audience in order to help them understand and prepare for an extreme weather emergency. The campaign deliverables have to be bilingual and specific to the city of Toronto. The focus isn’t to change behaviours that impact environment, but to create a community discussion, involvement, and resiliency to reduce vunerability in the event of a weather emergency. The goal here is to strengthen our immediate communitites.

My target audience are young adults, specifically millenials, due to the potential impact they can bring through various methods of communication including social media. Young Canadians have proven their trivial influence in the recent election where Liberals won majority.

The first deliverable is a poster which will attract the attention of young adults in Toronto and create a conversation about extreme weather preparedness. I chose to create a system for a series of posters comparing emergency preparedness to the simple tasks we perform on a regular basis in order to avoid common difficulties. The advertisment is intially absorbed with a sense of humour in order to capture the audience, and then relays a hard hitting message which will hopefully resonate with them.

Strolling Magazine

I’m currently working on a magazine for Editorial Design. We are to conceive a completely new magazine, including the identity, format, and genre.

Strolling magazine aims to stimulate young adults with an intriguing view on societal culture. The magazine uses photojournalism, literature, and experimental typography to capture the essence of the city featured in the issue. Unlike news journalism which discusses live events, Strolling investigates cultural realities that make up society.

The idea of strolling derives from the term Flânerie, the act of strolling. According to Charles Baudelaire, who coined the term, the flâneur is “a botanist of the sidewalk”. They stroll the city in order to experience the dynamics of the cty from a disengaged point of view. Another aesthetically attuned observer is the street photographer. All photographs showcased throughout the magazine are captured through black & white analog photography, with little to no re-touching. This beta issue will be featuring the city of Toronto.

I'm still experimenting with the aesthetic of the magazine. This is my most current grid structure and some sample spreads. I am also considering printing the magazine on a risograph to add some colour to the b&w photographs.
